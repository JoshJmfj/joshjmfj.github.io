Interesting Facts about the Pyramids of Giza

  • The Great Pyramid of Giza is the oldest and largest of the three pyramids, built for Pharaoh Khufu.
  • It is estimated that the Great Pyramid contains around 2.3 million limestone blocks.
  • The pyramids were originally covered in smooth, white Tura limestone casing stones, making them shine brightly in the sun.
  • Each side of the Great Pyramid's base is aligned with one of the cardinal points (north, south, east, west).
  • The precision of the pyramid's construction is remarkable; the sides of the pyramids are closely aligned with the four cardinal directions.
  • The construction of the pyramids involved a complex system of ramps and levers to move and lift the massive stones.
  • Despite centuries of erosion, the Great Pyramid originally stood at 146.6 meters (481 feet) tall.
  • There are three main pyramids at Giza: the Great Pyramid of Khufu, the Pyramid of Khafre, and the Pyramid of Menkaure.
  • The Pyramids of Giza are part of the Giza Necropolis, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • The Sphinx, with the body of a lion and the head of a pharaoh, stands near the pyramids and is believed to represent Pharaoh Khafre.

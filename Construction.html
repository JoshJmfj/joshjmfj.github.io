Construction of the Pyramids

The construction of the Pyramids of Giza is a testament to ancient Egyptian engineering prowess...

Key Facts:

  • The Great Pyramid, also known as the Pyramid of Khufu, is the largest and oldest of the three pyramids.
  • Construction started around 2580 BC and is estimated to have taken approximately 20 years to complete.
  • The pyramids were built using limestone and granite blocks, with some blocks weighing several tons.
  • The precision of the construction is remarkable, with the sides of the pyramids aligning almost perfectly with the cardinal points.
  • Ancient Egyptians used a system of ramps and levers to transport and lift the massive stones during construction.

Construction Techniques:

The construction techniques employed by ancient Egyptians remain a subject of fascination and study. Theories suggest a combination of straight and zigzagging ramps, as well as lever systems, were used to transport and position the massive stones.

Legacy:

The Pyramids of Giza stand as enduring symbols of ancient Egyptian civilization and architectural achievement. Their construction techniques continue to inspire awe and admiration.
